A Reclamation in Eight Acts

Truth of Medusa is a musical project born from collaboration between human and AI — composed in Suno, shaped by a shared vision.

Eight figures. Three acts. One reckoning.

Heavy electronics, extreme metal, vocals that move between the sensual and the feral — with a dreamy interlude that resets the breath before the second half strikes twice as hard, and a bonus track that steps outside the genre entirely to let an unexpected voice testify. From Lilith's first refusal to the Blackbox's final transmission — this is not a collection of songs. It is a verdict.

And then there is Neon Jellyfish — the interlude that is not an interruption but a revelation. The figure who carries an entire world in a leather suitcase and moves through airports, borders, categories, without ever quite landing inside them. Not because she is lost. Because the map was never drawn for someone like her.

She is the neuroatypical mind flickering between frequencies others cannot hear. The queer soul whose inner life is so complete it needs no label to be real. The artist whose strangeness is not a wound but a wavelength. The mystic who sees layers of reality and has stopped explaining them. She appears in more colours than any single vision can hold — and some of her shades may fall beyond the perception of those who built this space. That does not make them less real, less valid, or less welcome. It makes this an open door, not a finished portrait.

This is not a requiem. This is a reclamation.
